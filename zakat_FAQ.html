Frequently Asked Questions about Zakat

Common Questions and Answers

1. What is Zakat, and why is it important in Islam?

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars
  • Shahada: Faith
  • Salah: Prayer
  • Zakat: Charity
  • Sawm: Fasting during Ramadan
  • Hajj: Pilgrimage to Mecca
 of Islam. It is a mandatory act of charity that purifies a Muslim’s wealth and supports the poor and needy.

Reference: Qur'an, Surah Al-Baqarah (2:177); Sahih al-Bukhari, Volume 2, Book 24

2. Who is required to pay Zakat?

Zakat is obligatory for every adult Muslim (baligh) who possesses wealth above the Nisab (minimum threshold) and has held it for one lunar year. The individual must be sane and own wealth that exceeds basic needs.

Reference: Qur'an, Surah At-Tawbah (9:60)

3. How do I calculate Zakat on my savings, gold, or property?

Zakat is generally calculated as 2.5% of one’s total wealth if it exceeds the Nisab. Savings and gold are included if held for one lunar year. Real estate used for living is exempt, but investment property is subject to Zakat.

Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari, Volume 2, Book 24; Hanafi Fiqh

4. What percentage of my wealth do I need to pay as Zakat?

Zakat is 2.5% of qualifying wealth that has been held for one lunar year. This includes savings, gold, business assets, and investments.

Reference: Sahih Muslim (Book 12); Qur'an, Surah Al-Baqarah (2:267)

5. Who can receive Zakat?

Zakat can be distributed to eight categories of people as mentioned in the Qur'an: the poor, the needy, those employed to collect Zakat, new converts to Islam, those in bondage or debt, in the path of Allah, and travelers.

Reference: Qur'an, Surah At-Tawbah (9:60)

6. Can I give Zakat to my family members?

Zakat cannot be given to immediate family members for whom you are responsible (like parents, children, or spouse). However, extended family members (such as siblings, aunts, or uncles) can receive Zakat if they meet the eligibility criteria.

Reference: Fiqh al-Zakat by Yusuf al-Qaradawi

7. Is Zakat applicable on business income or investments?

Yes, Zakat is applicable on business assets and investments if they meet the Nisab. This includes goods for sale, receivables, and profits.

Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari, Volume 2, Book 24; Hanafi Fiqh

8. When is Zakat due?

Zakat is due once a lunar year has passed on wealth that meets or exceeds the Nisab (minimum threshold) for a full lunar year. To explain this better, let’s walk through an example:

Example: Starting Zakat Today

Let’s say you decide to start paying Zakat today (September 23, 2024). Here are the steps you’d follow to determine when to pay Zakat:

Step 1: Establish Your Zakat Due Date (Hawl)

Zakat operates on the Hawl, which is a full lunar year (about 354 days). You need to fix the starting date on which you begin tracking your wealth. Let’s assume you start today, on September 23, 2024 (which is Rabi' al-Thani 9, 1446 AH in the Islamic calendar).

Step 2: Check the Nisab

The Nisab is the threshold of wealth that makes Zakat obligatory. For gold, it’s 87.48 grams, and for silver, it’s 612.36 grams. Alternatively, some scholars recommend using a monetary equivalent of either.

For example, if you have savings, investments, and gold, you need to calculate the total amount of wealth in today's value and see if it exceeds the Nisab.

If your wealth exceeds the Nisab on September 23, 2024, then the countdown for the lunar year begins from that date. If your wealth is below the Nisab, you wait until it exceeds the threshold and mark that as your Zakat date.

Step 3: Maintain Your Wealth Over One Lunar Year

If your wealth stays above the Nisab for a full lunar year, Zakat becomes due at the end of the lunar year.

Your lunar year would end around September 11, 2025 (roughly 354 days later, corresponding to Rabi' al-Thani 1, 1447 AH).

At that time, you will calculate all of your eligible wealth (savings, investments, gold, etc.) and pay 2.5% of it as Zakat.

Step 4: Calculation at the End of the Lunar Year

On September 11, 2025, you will calculate your total wealth. This includes:

  • Cash savings
  • Business income
  • Gold, silver
  • Investments (stocks, mutual funds, etc.)
  • Any outstanding loans that you expect to receive

If the total still exceeds the Nisab, you must pay 2.5% of that total as Zakat.

Step 5: Paying Zakat

Zakat can be paid anytime after the lunar year has completed, and it's recommended to pay as soon as possible. Some people prefer paying during Ramadan for added blessings, but this is not a requirement. You can also choose to pay Zakat in installments throughout the year as long as the full amount is paid.

Example Breakdown

Starting date: September 23, 2024 (Rabi' al-Thani 9, 1446 AH)
Lunar year end: September 11, 2025 (Rabi' al-Thani 1, 1447 AH)
Amount to pay: 2.5% of your wealth at the end of the year, provided it’s above the Nisab.

Things to Check:
  • Ensure that your wealth has stayed above the Nisab for the full lunar year.
  • Calculate the Zakat accurately by considering your savings, gold, business assets, etc.
  • Make sure you pay it promptly after the lunar year ends.

Reference: Sunan Ibn Majah (1792)

9. Can Zakat be paid in installments?

Yes, Zakat can be paid in installments as long as the full amount is paid within the lunar year. It is recommended, however, to pay it all at once if possible to meet the immediate needs of the recipients.

Reference: Fiqh al-Zakat by Yusuf al-Qaradawi
