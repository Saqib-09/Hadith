FAQs on Sacrifice (Qurbani) for Eid ul-Adha

Common Questions and Answers

1. What is the significance of sacrifice on Eid ul-Adha?

The sacrifice on Eid ul-Adha commemorates the willingness of Prophet Ibrahim (AS) to sacrifice his son Ismail (AS) in obedience to Allah’s command. It is an act of devotion, symbolizing submission to Allah's will.

Reference: Qur'an, Surah As-Saffat (37:102-107)

2. Who is obligated to perform the sacrifice?

Sacrifice is obligatory (wajib) for every adult Muslim who meets the Nisab (minimum wealth threshold), similar to Zakat. If a person has enough wealth, they are required to offer a sacrifice during Eid ul-Adha.

Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari (Volume 7, Book 68), Fiqh rulings from the Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, and Hanbali schools of thought.

3. What types of animals are eligible for sacrifice?

Eligible animals for sacrifice include sheep, goats (at least one year old), cows or bulls (at least two years old), and camels (at least five years old). The animal must be healthy and free from defects.

Reference: Sahih Muslim (Book 22, Hadith 4844)

4. How is the sacrifice performed?

The sacrifice is performed by slaughtering the animal humanely, with the name of Allah (Bismillah, Allahu Akbar) being pronounced. The animal’s throat is cut swiftly to ensure a quick death, minimizing suffering.

Reference: Sahih Muslim (Book 22, Hadith 4862)

5. When should the sacrifice be made?

The sacrifice is performed after the Eid ul-Adha prayer, from the 10th of Dhul-Hijjah to the sunset of the 13th. It should not be performed before the Eid prayer.

Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari (Volume 7, Book 68, Hadith 453)

6. How should the meat from the sacrifice be distributed?

The meat is traditionally divided into three parts: one-third for the family, one-third for relatives or friends, and one-third for the poor and needy.

Reference: Sahih al-Bukhari (Volume 7, Book 68)

7. Is it permissible to offer the sacrifice on behalf of someone else?

Yes, you can offer a sacrifice on behalf of others, such as family members or deceased relatives. However, they must give explicit permission for the sacrifice to fulfill their obligation.

Reference: Sahih Muslim (Book 22, Hadith 4862)

8. What should be avoided during the days leading up to the sacrifice?

Those intending to perform the sacrifice are advised to refrain from cutting their hair, nails, or any part of the body from the 1st of Dhul-Hijjah until the sacrifice is completed. This is a recommended Sunnah.

Reference: Sahih Muslim (Book 22, Hadith 4870)

9. Can a non-working woman or a working woman offer sacrifice even if her husband intends to perform it?

In the Hanafi school, if a woman (whether working or non-working) meets the Nisab value, she is individually obligated to perform the sacrifice, even if her husband is offering one for the family. The sacrifice obligation is based on wealth ownership, and each person above the Nisab must offer their own sacrifice.

However, in the Shafi'i, Maliki, and Hanbali schools, one sacrifice from the head of the household suffices for the whole family, but if the woman wishes, she may still perform her own sacrifice. The obligation is not as strict in these schools, where Qurbani is **Sunnah Mu'akkadah** (highly recommended).

Reference: Hanafi: Sahih al-Bukhari (Volume 7, Book 68), Fiqh rulings; Shafi'i, Maliki, Hanbali: Fiqh rulings from classical scholars.

10. If a non-working woman does not offer sacrifice even though she meets the Nisab value, is she sinful?

According to the Hanafi school, if a non-working woman possesses wealth above the Nisab and does not perform the sacrifice without a valid excuse, she may be considered sinful. This is because Qurbani is wajib (obligatory) for those who meet the Nisab, regardless of their employment status. However, in the Shafi'i, Maliki, and Hanbali schools of thought, Qurbani is Sunnah Mu'akkadah (highly recommended), and while it is strongly encouraged, it is not considered sinful if missed.

Reference: Hanafi: Sahih al-Bukhari (Volume 7, Book 68), Fiqh al-Zakat; Shafi'i, Maliki, Hanbali: Fiqh rulings from classical scholars.

11. If a family has multiple members who all meet the Nisab value, do all of them need to offer a sacrifice?

According to the Hanafi school, each individual above the Nisab must offer their own sacrifice. However, the other schools (Shafi'i, Maliki, Hanbali) permit one sacrifice per family, though individuals may perform their own if they wish.

Reference: Hanafi: Sahih Muslim (Book 22, Hadith 4844); Shafi'i, Maliki, Hanbali: Fiqh rulings from classical scholars.
